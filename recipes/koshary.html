Koshary

45 min

Medium

A classic Egyptian dish, Koshary is a delightful blend of lentils, rice, pasta, and a flavorful tomato sauce, topped with crispy fried onions and chickpeas.

1 cup Lentils
1 cup White rice
1 cup Short pasta (e.g., elbow or penne)
3 tbsp Vegetable oil
1 Large onion, thinly sliced
1 can (14 oz) Chickpeas, drained and rinsed
2 cups Tomato sauce
Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation

Step 1: Cook the lentils, rice, and pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

Step 2: In a large pan, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add thinly sliced onions and cook until golden brown and crispy. Remove half of the crispy onions for garnish.

Step 3: In the same pan, add chickpeas and cook until they become slightly crispy. Remove half for garnish alongside the onions.

Step 4: Add the tomato sauce to the pan, season with salt and pepper, and let it simmer for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

Step 5: Mix the cooked lentils, rice, and pasta together. Serve the mixture in bowls, topping it with the tomato sauce. Garnish each serving with the reserved crispy onions and chickpeas.
